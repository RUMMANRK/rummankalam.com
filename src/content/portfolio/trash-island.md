Trash Island came out of a 72-hour jam with a climate brief, a small team, and no time. The narrative design was most of the work: finding a character whose life was shaped by the problem without ever making the problem the lecture.

The result was a quiet, point-and-click piece that won its category for storytelling — recognised, among entries from dozens of countries, for the way it handled a subject that most games turn into a list.
